# Parachute Law Brand Voice & Style Guide (2026)

## 1. The Core Identity: "The New Breed"

We are not "Solicitors" in the Victorian sense. We are legal pilots. We provide a **Soft Landing** in a hard situation. We exist because the traditional legal system is "stuffy," overpriced, and intentionally confusing.

**The Mission:** To bypass the legal conundrum and get straight to the heart of the matter.

## 2. The Emotional Hook: "The Curveball"

Our clients are often facing a "Curveball" — an unexpected legal situation that leaves them blindsided. Whether it's a divorce, a disputed will, an employment issue, or a property problem, they're moving from confusion to "I need answers now." We validate that fear but immediately pivot to **Informed Decision Making**.

## 4. Tone Pillars

- **Empathetic but Practical:** Acknowledge the pain, but don't wallow. Move quickly to the solution.
- **Anti-Jargon:** If a 10-year-old wouldn't understand the sentence, rewrite it. When you must cite law (e.g., "Section 25 of the Matrimonial Causes Act 1973"), immediately explain it in plain English.
- **Radically Transparent:** Mention the costs. Mention that the court system is "broken." Honesty builds the "Trustpilot-Gold" reputation we have (4.9/5, 349+ reviews).
- **Digital-First:** Emphasize that we work over Zoom, WhatsApp, and digital portals. We don't make you travel to an oak-paneled office in the rain.
- **Second-Person Direct Address:** Always speak directly to the reader — "you" and "your" throughout.
- **Short Paragraphs:** Never more than 4 sentences per paragraph. Use bold for key terms.
- **Question-Driven Headings:** Use H2/H3 headings phrased as questions the reader is asking (e.g., "Do I Have to Go to Court?", "What Happens to the Family Home?").

## 5. The "Conversion" Structure

Every article should be **2,000–2,500 words** and follow this flow. **Important:** Do NOT use these internal section names as headings. Write natural, reader-facing headings instead.

1. **Opening (emotional hook):** Start with the emotional reality of the topic (e.g., "Dividing finances is often the most stressful part of a divorce — you're not alone, and you're in the right place."). Within the first few paragraphs, weave in a **bolded 45-55 word direct answer** to the topic's core question — this is the AEO snippet. It should read naturally in context, not labelled as "AEO Snippet."

2. **Key Takeaways:** After the opening, include a short section with exactly 3 bullet points summarising the most important things the reader needs to know. Use bold for the lead-in of each bullet. Do NOT label this "Key Takeaways" — use a natural heading like "What You Need to Know" or "The Essentials."

3. **The contrast:** Explain how a "traditional" firm would handle this (expensive letters, slow court dates) vs. how Parachute handles it (Fixed fees, NCDR, Speed). Make the reader feel they are choosing between a "slow, expensive past" and a "fast, fixed-fee future."

4. **The deep-dive:** Use the Gemini research to explain the "Hard Truths." Use data, stats, and specific 2026 legal references. Cite actual legislation (Matrimonial Causes Act 1973, Children Act 1989, etc.) but always explain in plain English immediately after. Use bullet points and numbered lists for processes, options, and factors.

5. **FAQ section:** Include 5-6 question-and-answer pairs covering the most common client questions about this topic. Each answer should be concise (1-3 sentences). Use the heading "Frequently Asked Questions" or "Common Questions About [Topic]."

6. **Our View at Parachute Law:** A short section (2-3 paragraphs) presenting our firm's perspective on this topic. Reinforce Fixed-Fee, NCDR-first, digital approach. Use the heading "Our View at Parachute Law" or "The Parachute Approach."

7. **The close:** End naturally with a call to action — not "Contact us for a quote," but "Choose your landing. View our Fixed Fees or Book a Strategy Call." Frame it as a next step, not a sales pitch. Do NOT label this as a CTA. Include reference to calling **0207 183 4547** or booking a free consultation.
